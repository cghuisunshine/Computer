Classification Lab

In this lesson, AI does not predict a number. Instead, it chooses a label. Examples: cat or dog, spam or not spam.

Image clue Cat-like features can help the AI choose the label “cat”.
Image clue Dog-like features can help the AI choose the label “dog”.

What is classification? Classification means putting something into a group or category.
Main idea The AI looks at features, gives each label a score, and chooses the highest score.
Your mission Test simple classifiers and see how AI decides between labels.

1. Quick Review: Regression vs Classification

Regression Predicts a number, like a score, price, or temperature.
Classification Predicts a label, like cat, dog, spam, or not spam.
Features Features are clues the AI uses, such as “has whiskers” or “contains FREE”.
1Look at features
2Give each label a score
3Compare scores
4Choose a label

A classifier does not “know” like a person. It uses patterns from examples and makes its best guess.

5. Why Classification Can Make Mistakes

Too few features If the AI does not get enough clues, it may guess the wrong label.
Confusing examples Some cases look similar. A classifier may not be 100% sure.
Biased training data If the training examples are poor, the model can learn bad patterns.

Real AI systems should be checked by humans, especially when the decision is important.
